Crop-Specific Feeding Guide

Feed your soil, not your plants. Match your crop feed categories to specific compost and organic soil amendments.

🍅 Heavy Feeders
High N-P-K + Calcium

Crops: Tomatoes, Peppers, Zucchini, Broccoli, Cabbage, Corn.
Soil Strategy: Need lots of nitrogen for growth, and high calcium/phosphorus to prevent Blossom-End Rot. Dig in 2-3 inches of compost and add bone meal or agricultural gypsum before planting.

🥕 Root Developers
Low Nitrogen, High P-K

Crops: Carrots, Potatoes, Beets, Radishes, Onions.
Soil Strategy: Too much nitrogen causes leafy tops but tiny/hairy roots. Focus on Potassium and Phosphorus for root enlargement. Amend soil with composted manure, wood ash, or organic kelp meal.

🥬 Leafy Greens
High Nitrogen, Low P

Crops: Lettuce, Spinach, Arugula, Herbs.
Soil Strategy: Fast-growing leafy crops need accessible nitrogen. Side-dress crops with alfalfa meal, feather meal, or leaf-mold compost mid-season.

🫛 Soil Builders
Nitrogen Fixers

Crops: Beans, Peas, Clover, Vetch.
Soil Strategy: Symbiotic roots host Rhizobia bacteria to fix atmospheric nitrogen directly. Do not fertilize with nitrogen; add light compost. Inoculate seeds before planting to maximize soil building.
